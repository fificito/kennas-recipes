Dried Wild Mushroom Risotto

Category: Main Course

Ingredients

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 shallot, finely minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4-5 cups warm vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup dried wild mushrooms (porcini, morels, etc.), soaked in hot water
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Reserved mushroom soaking liquid (strained)

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried wild mushrooms in hot water for about 20-30 minutes. Strain the liquid and reserve it. Finely chop the mushrooms.
  2. In a large pot or skillet, melt 1 tbsp of butter and 1 tbsp of olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the minced shallot and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Stir in the chopped dried mushrooms and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the arborio rice and toast it for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the edges are translucent.
  6. Pour in the white wine and stir until it's fully absorbed.
  7. Begin adding the warm broth and reserved mushroom liquid one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until each addition is absorbed before adding more.
  8. Continue this process for about 18-22 minutes until the rice is tender but still has a slight bite (al dente).
  9. Remove from heat. Stir in the remaining butter, parmesan cheese, and fresh parsley.
  10. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Serve immediately.
